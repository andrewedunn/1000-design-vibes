typography

cormorant garamond

Refined serif headings in lowercase for understated elegance

Georgia body text provides excellent readability while maintaining the refined aesthetic. The system uses tight to normal letter spacing, supporting the compact, focused nature of nonprofit communication. Line height remains conservative at 1.5-1.6 to maximize the airy density principle.

neumorphic surfaces

inset depth

Soft inset shadows create a recessed button-like appearance, inviting interaction without harsh edges. Perfect for nonprofit interfaces seeking approachability.

organic radius

20px border radius softens surfaces while maintaining geometric precision. The rounded corners support the organic color palette seamlessly.

floating effect

Combined inset and outset shadows create subtle dimensionality. Surfaces feel hand-crafted rather than rigidly digital.

design principles

restraint & elegance

Lowercase typography and muted earth tones speak of understated confidence. This is design that whispers rather than shouts.

tactile warmth

Neumorphic surfaces with paper and fabric texture references create a handmade feeling. Users want to trust nonprofit design—this system delivers that through familiar, craft-based aesthetics.

airy composition

With 10px base spacing, components breathe. Nothing feels crowded or aggressive. This density approach supports meditation and careful consideration—ideal for donor-facing and mission-critical interfaces.
