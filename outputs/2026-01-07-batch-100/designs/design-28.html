Design Philosophy

Mizu Winter combines Material Design's layered elevation philosophy with Japanese sensibility for restraint and negative space. The Y2K influence emerges through geometric precision and playful proportions, while the dark monochromatic color palette maintains focus and reduces cognitive load.

Very loose letter-spacing creates breathing room. Pill-shaped corners appear friendly without sacrificing professionalism. Inset shadows suggest depth without floating surfaces. This is a system for interfaces that feel both technical and human.
