Organic System

Flowing Curves

Art nouveau-inspired organic shapes reference nature. Children connect with curves more than rigid angles.

Double Borders

Art nouveau decorative borders add ornamental charm. Double lines create visual richness and playfulness.

Neon Energy

Hyper-saturated colors pop against dark backgrounds. Vivid triadic palette maintains children's visual engagement.

Duotone Depth

Gradient fills add dimensionality without shadows. Compact tight letter spacing (tight) supports dense information.

Edgy Personality

Art nouveau crosses with punk attitude. Educational content doesn't need to be bland or corporate.

Centered Harmony

Center alignment creates balanced, meditative layouts. Children find comfort in symmetry and visual order.

~ ~ ~

Typography System

Caveat Script

Handwritten serif creates personal, welcoming tone. Children feel spoken to directly, not lectured. Casual handwriting builds trust.

Courier Prime Monospace

Typewriter aesthetic adds nostalgic charm and clarity. Tight line height (normal) keeps dense nonprofit content organized.

Playful Constraint

Compact 4px base unit forces intentional design. But neon colors and curved shapes prevent any sense of claustrophobia.

edgy art for edgy kids!

Design Principles

Nature Meets Neon

Art nouveau celebrates organic beauty. Neon colors bring modern energy. Together: nature isn't boring—it's vibrant and exciting.

Educational Excitement

Nonprofits teaching children need visual interest. Flowery art nouveau curves and shocking neon colors command attention without feeling commercial.

Whimsical Structure

Double borders, ornamental elements, and flowing curves create visual richness. Yet center alignment and whitespace maintain clarity for young readers.
