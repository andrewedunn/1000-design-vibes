Celebrating a Life Well-Lived

Remembering the extraordinary journey of Margaret

Margaret Eleanor Thompson passed away peacefully on December 28, 2025, at the age of 83, surrounded by her loving family. Born in Portland, Oregon, Margaret was the beloved daughter of Robert and Eleanor Davis, and grew up with a deep appreciation for nature, literature, and community service.

After graduating from Reed College with honors in English Literature, Margaret dedicated 35 years of her life to teaching high school English, touching the lives of thousands of students with her passion for poetry and storytelling. Her classroom was a sanctuary where Shakespeare came alive and young minds discovered the power of words.

Margaret married the love of her life, William Thompson, in 1965, and together they built a beautiful life filled with adventure, laughter, and unconditional love. She was a devoted mother to three children‚ÄîSarah, Michael, and Jennifer‚Äîand a cherished grandmother to seven grandchildren who affectionately called her "Grammy."

An avid gardener, Margaret's rose garden was legendary in the neighborhood, winning multiple awards from the local horticultural society. She was also an accomplished pianist, active volunteer at the local library, and passionate advocate for literacy programs. Her warm smile, generous spirit, and unwavering kindness left an indelible mark on everyone fortunate enough to know her.

Margaret is survived by her husband William of 60 years; children Sarah (John) Mitchell, Michael (Lisa) Thompson, and Jennifer (David) Reynolds; grandchildren Emma, Nathan, Olivia, Samuel, Grace, Benjamin, and Charlotte; and her sister Patricia Davis. She was preceded in death by her parents and brother Thomas Davis.
