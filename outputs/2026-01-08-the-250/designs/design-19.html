Financial Analysis & ROI Calculations

Master the essential financial metrics every real estate investor needs to know. Learn how to calculate return on investment, cash-on-cash returns, and evaluate property performance.

10:24 / 28:00

Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Calculate key financial metrics including Cap Rate, Cash-on-Cash Return, and Internal Rate of Return (IRR)
  • Analyze property cash flow and identify potential income streams
  • Evaluate investment opportunities using the 1% and 2% rules
  • Create pro forma financial statements for investment properties
  • Understand the impact of leverage on investment returns

Key Concepts

1. Capitalization Rate (Cap Rate)

The cap rate is one of the most fundamental metrics in real estate investing. It represents the rate of return on a real estate investment property based on the income that the property is expected to generate. The formula is:

Cap Rate = Net Operating Income (NOI) / Property Value × 100

A higher cap rate typically indicates a potentially better return on investment, but may also signal higher risk. Cap rates vary significantly by market, property type, and condition.

Example: A property with an NOI of $45,000 and a purchase price of $500,000 has a cap rate of 9% ($45,000 / $500,000 = 0.09 or 9%).

2. Cash-on-Cash Return

Cash-on-cash return measures the annual pre-tax cash flow relative to the amount of cash invested. This metric is particularly useful when comparing leveraged investments because it accounts for mortgage payments.

Cash-on-Cash Return = Annual Pre-Tax Cash Flow / Total Cash Invested × 100

3. Net Operating Income (NOI)

NOI is the foundation of most real estate financial analysis. It represents the property's annual income after operating expenses but before debt service and capital expenditures. Calculate it as:

  • Gross Rental Income
  • - Vacancy Loss
  • - Operating Expenses (property taxes, insurance, maintenance, property management)
  • = Net Operating Income

4. The 1% and 2% Rules

These are quick screening tools to evaluate rental properties:

  • 1% Rule: Monthly rent should be at least 1% of the purchase price
  • 2% Rule: Monthly rent should be at least 2% of the purchase price

While not definitive, properties that meet these thresholds generally warrant deeper analysis. The 2% rule is more aggressive and common in lower-cost markets.

Practical Application

In the video above, we walked through a complete financial analysis of a sample property: a three-unit multifamily building listed at $425,000. We calculated:

  • Projected gross rental income: $3,600/month ($1,200 per unit)
  • Estimated vacancy loss: 5% ($2,160/year)
  • Annual operating expenses: $12,800
  • Net Operating Income: $28,240
  • Cap Rate: 6.64%
  • Cash-on-Cash Return (with 20% down, 6% interest): 8.2%

This analysis revealed that while the property doesn't meet the 1% rule strictly, the strong cap rate and cash-on-cash return in this appreciating market make it a viable investment opportunity.
