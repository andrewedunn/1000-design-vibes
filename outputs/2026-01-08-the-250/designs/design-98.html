Classic Three-Meat Lasagna

A rich, layered masterpiece featuring ground beef, Italian sausage, and three premium cheeses. This traditional lasagna delivers deep, savory flavors with a perfectly balanced meat-to-cheese ratio. The secret lies in the slow-simmered sauce and strategic layering technique that ensures every bite is perfectly proportioned.

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add ground beef and Italian sausage, breaking up meat with a wooden spoon. Cook until deeply browned, approximately 8-10 minutes. Remove meat and set aside, leaving 2 tablespoons of fat in the pot.

  2. Reduce heat to medium. Add diced onion and cook until softened and translucent, about 5 minutes. Add minced garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute. Stir in tomato paste and cook for 2 minutes to caramelize.

    Don't skip the tomato paste caramelization step—it adds critical depth to the sauce.

  3. Return browned meat to the pot. Add crushed tomatoes, oregano, red pepper flakes, salt, and pepper. Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat to low. Cook uncovered for 30-40 minutes, stirring occasionally, until sauce thickens and flavors meld.

  4. While sauce simmers, prepare the cheese mixture. In a large bowl, combine ricotta, egg, half the Parmigiano-Reggiano, half the torn basil, ½ teaspoon salt, and ¼ teaspoon black pepper. Mix until smooth and well incorporated.

  5. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Spread 1 cup of meat sauce across the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish. Layer 3-4 pasta sheets, overlapping slightly. Spread one-third of the ricotta mixture, then one-quarter of the mozzarella. Top with 1½ cups meat sauce.

    Ensure pasta sheets overlap by about ½ inch to prevent gaps during baking.

  6. Repeat layering process two more times: pasta, ricotta mixture, mozzarella, meat sauce. For the final layer, add pasta sheets, remaining meat sauce, remaining mozzarella, and finish with remaining Parmigiano-Reggiano.

  7. Cover tightly with aluminum foil (spray foil with cooking spray to prevent cheese sticking). Bake for 45 minutes. Remove foil and bake an additional 15 minutes until top is golden brown and edges are bubbling vigorously.

  8. Remove from oven and let rest for 15-20 minutes before slicing. This crucial step allows the layers to set, ensuring clean cuts. Garnish with remaining torn basil leaves and serve immediately.

    Patience is key—cutting too early results in a sloppy presentation. The wait is worth it.

Chef Notes

  • For best results, use high-quality San Marzano tomatoes—their natural sweetness balances the richness of the meat
  • Fresh pasta sheets produce superior texture compared to dried noodles and don't require pre-cooking
  • This lasagna freezes beautifully for up to 3 months; assemble completely, wrap tightly, and bake from frozen (add 30 minutes)
  • Day-old lasagna often tastes even better as flavors continue to develop; reheat covered at 350°F
  • For a lighter version, substitute half the ground beef with ground turkey—maintain the sausage for flavor
