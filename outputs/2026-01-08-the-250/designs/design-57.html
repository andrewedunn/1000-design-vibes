Classic Spaghetti Carbonara

Authentic Roman pasta with eggs, guanciale, and Pecorino Romano

Prep Time 15 min
Cook Time 20 min
Servings 4
Difficulty Medium
Italian Pasta Main Course Medium
5.0 (324 reviews)

Ingredients

400g Spaghetti
200g Guanciale, diced
4 large Egg yolks
1 whole Egg
100g Pecorino Romano, finely grated
to taste Black pepper, freshly ground
to taste Salt, for pasta water

Instructions

01
Bring a large pot of salted water to boil. The water should taste like the sea. Add spaghetti and cook until al dente according to package directions, typically 8-10 minutes.
02
While pasta cooks, add diced guanciale to a cold large skillet. Turn heat to medium and cook until fat renders and guanciale is crispy, about 8-10 minutes. Remove from heat.
03
In a bowl, whisk together egg yolks, whole egg, grated Pecorino Romano, and a generous amount of black pepper. Mix until smooth and creamy.
04
Reserve 1 cup of pasta cooking water. Drain spaghetti and immediately add to the skillet with guanciale. Toss to combine while still hot.
05
Remove skillet from heat. Add egg mixture and toss vigorously, adding pasta water gradually until sauce is creamy and coats pasta. The residual heat will cook the eggs without scrambling.
06
Serve immediately with extra Pecorino Romano and black pepper on top. Carbonara waits for no one—enjoy while hot and creamy.

Chef's Notes
Key to success: The secret to perfect carbonara is temperature control. The egg mixture must be added off-heat to prevent scrambling, using only the residual heat from the pasta and guanciale. Traditional Roman carbonara never uses cream—the creaminess comes entirely from the emulsified eggs and cheese. If you can't find guanciale, pancetta is an acceptable substitute, though purists may disagree. Never use bacon as it's too smoky.
