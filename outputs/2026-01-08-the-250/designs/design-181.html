Article I: Purpose and Scope

1.1
Agreement Purpose

This Patient Consent and Treatment Authorization Agreement ("Agreement") establishes the legal framework governing the healthcare services relationship between Crisp Prime Medical Center and the Patient. This Agreement covers all aspects of care delivery, privacy protections, billing procedures, and mutual responsibilities as required by federal and state healthcare regulations.

1.2
Scope of Services

The Provider agrees to deliver healthcare services within their scope of practice, including but not limited to:

(a) Primary care consultations, examinations, and diagnostic services;
(b) Preventive care services including vaccinations and health screenings;
(c) Treatment planning, medication management, and therapeutic interventions;
(d) Referral coordination for specialist care and ancillary services;
(e) Health education, wellness counseling, and disease prevention guidance.
1.3
Patient Acknowledgment

The Patient acknowledges that they have been provided with adequate information regarding their medical condition, proposed treatment options, associated risks and benefits, and alternative approaches. The Patient voluntarily consents to receive healthcare services from the Provider and their designated clinical staff.

Definitions

"Protected Health Information" (PHI) means any individually identifiable health information transmitted or maintained in any form or medium by the Provider, as defined under HIPAA regulations 45 CFR § 160.103.
"Medical Necessity" means healthcare services or supplies needed to diagnose or treat an illness, injury, condition, disease, or its symptoms, meeting accepted standards of medical practice.
"Emergency Care" means healthcare services provided to evaluate or stabilize a medical condition that manifests by acute symptoms of sufficient severity such that absence of immediate medical attention could reasonably result in serious jeopardy to the patient's health.

Article II: Privacy and Confidentiality

2.1
HIPAA Authorization

The Patient authorizes the Provider to use and disclose their Protected Health Information for purposes of treatment, payment, and healthcare operations as permitted under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) and implementing regulations. The Patient acknowledges receipt of the Provider's Notice of Privacy Practices, which describes in detail how medical information may be used and disclosed.

2.2
Information Sharing

The Patient consents to the sharing of their medical information with:

(a) Healthcare providers involved in the Patient's care and treatment;
(b) Insurance companies and third-party payers for billing and payment processing;
(c) Laboratories, imaging centers, and other diagnostic service providers;
(d) Public health authorities as required by law for disease reporting and prevention.
2.3
Patient Rights

The Patient retains the right to request restrictions on certain uses and disclosures of their medical information, access their medical records, request amendments to inaccurate information, and receive an accounting of disclosures as provided under federal privacy regulations. Such requests must be submitted in writing to the Provider's Privacy Officer.

Article III: Financial Responsibility

3.1
Payment Obligation

The Patient agrees to be financially responsible for all charges incurred for healthcare services provided, including but not limited to professional fees, facility charges, laboratory and diagnostic services, medications, and medical supplies. Payment is due upon receipt of statement unless other arrangements have been made with the Provider's billing department.

3.2
Insurance Assignment

The Patient assigns all insurance benefits and payments directly to the Provider. The Patient authorizes the Provider to file claims with their insurance company and release any necessary medical information for payment processing. The Patient remains responsible for any amounts not covered by insurance, including deductibles, co-payments, and non-covered services.

Article IV: Treatment Consent

4.1
General Consent to Treat

The Patient hereby consents to medical examination, diagnostic procedures, and treatment by the Provider and their clinical staff. This consent includes routine healthcare services deemed medically necessary by the Provider in their professional judgment. The Patient understands that medicine is not an exact science and acknowledges that no guarantees have been made regarding treatment outcomes.

4.2
Right to Refuse

The Patient retains the right to refuse any examination, procedure, or treatment. The Patient acknowledges that such refusal may impact their health outcomes and that the Provider has explained the potential consequences of refusing recommended medical care. The Provider reserves the right to decline to provide care if the Patient's refusal prevents the delivery of safe and effective treatment.
