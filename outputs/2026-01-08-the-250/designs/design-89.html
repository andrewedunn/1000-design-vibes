Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common travel documentation questions

For international travel, you typically need: (1) A valid passport with at least 6 months validity beyond your travel dates, (2) Appropriate visa if required by your destination country, (3) Return or onward ticket proof, (4) Proof of accommodation, (5) Travel insurance documentation, and (6) Any required health certificates or vaccination records.

Requirements vary significantly by destination and nationality. Always verify specific requirements for your destination country at least 3 months before travel.

Modifications and cancellations depend on your fare type. Most bookings can be managed through your account dashboard. Navigate to "My Trips," select your reservation, and choose "Modify" or "Cancel."

Flexible fares typically allow free changes up to 24 hours before departure. Standard fares may incur modification fees ranging from $50-$200. Non-refundable fares cannot be cancelled for cash refunds but may offer travel credits.

For bookings made within the last 24 hours, you may qualify for free cancellation under DOT regulations (for US departures).

Prohibited carry-on items include: Liquids exceeding 100ml (3.4oz) per container, sharp objects (knives, scissors over 4 inches), tools, sporting equipment that could be used as weapons, firearms and ammunition, explosives and flammable items, and self-defense items.

Liquids must be in containers of 100ml or less, placed in a single transparent, resealable 1-liter bag. Each passenger is limited to one liquids bag.

Exceptions apply for medications, baby formula, and special dietary needs with proper documentation.

Recommended arrival times: Domestic flights - 2 hours before departure, International flights - 3 hours before departure, Peak travel periods (holidays) - Add 30-60 minutes to above times.

These recommendations account for check-in, security screening, and boarding processes. During busy periods, security lines can exceed 45 minutes at major airports.

To file an insurance claim: (1) Contact your insurance provider within 24 hours of the incident, (2) Document everything - photos, receipts, medical reports, police reports if applicable, (3) Complete the claims form through your provider's portal, (4) Submit all supporting documentation, (5) Retain copies of everything submitted.

Most claims are processed within 10-15 business days for straightforward cases. Complex claims involving medical issues may take 30-45 days.

In case of delays or cancellations: Airlines must rebook you on the next available flight at no additional cost, provide meal vouchers for delays exceeding 3 hours, and arrange accommodation for overnight delays when the delay is within airline control.

EU Regulation 261/2004 provides compensation for EU flights ranging from €250-€600 depending on distance and delay duration. US DOT rules require refunds for significant schedule changes.

Always request written documentation of the delay reason and keep all receipts for reasonable expenses incurred.
