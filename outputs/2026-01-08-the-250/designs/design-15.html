Creating Your Starter

5 days

A sourdough starter is a living culture of wild yeast and beneficial bacteria. This natural leavening agent gives sourdough its characteristic tangy flavor and chewy texture. Building a strong starter takes time, but the results are worth the patience.

Initial Mixture
  • 50g whole wheat flour (for wild yeast)
  • 50g filtered water (room temperature)
  • Clean glass jar (at least 500ml capacity)

Instructions: Mix flour and water in your jar until no dry flour remains. The consistency should be thick but stirrable, like pancake batter. Cover loosely with a cloth or lid (don't seal tightly—the starter needs to breathe). Store at room temperature, away from direct sunlight.

Temperature Matters
Keep your starter between 70-75°F (21-24°C). Warmer temperatures speed up fermentation, while cooler temps slow it down. Consistent temperature yields the most reliable results.
2

Daily Feeding Routine

10 min/day

For the next 5-7 days, you'll feed your starter daily. This process removes old waste products and provides fresh food for the yeast and bacteria. Watch for bubbles and a pleasant sour smell—these are signs of healthy fermentation.

Daily Feeding Recipe
  • Discard half of your starter (about 50g)
  • Add 50g all-purpose flour
  • Add 50g filtered water
  • Stir thoroughly until combined

What to Look For:

  • Day 1-2: Little to no activity—this is normal
  • Day 3-4: Small bubbles appear, slight sour smell develops
  • Day 5-7: Starter doubles in size between feedings, lots of bubbles
Track Your Progress

Mark the starter level on your jar with a rubber band or tape after feeding. Check 4-6 hours later to see if it's doubled.

Pro Tip
Save your discarded starter! Use it for pancakes, crackers, or waffles. There are countless recipes that put this "waste" to delicious use.

Checkpoint: Is Your Starter Ready?

Before moving to the next step, verify your starter meets these criteria:

  • Doubles in size within 4-6 hours after feeding
  • Has a pleasant, tangy (not unpleasant) smell
  • Shows lots of bubbles throughout, not just on top
  • Passes the float test (a spoonful floats in water)
  • Has been consistently active for at least 2 days

Not quite there? Keep feeding daily. Some starters take 7-10 days to become fully active. Patience is key!
