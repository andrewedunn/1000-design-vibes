The Renaissance of Urban Gardens in Modern Architecture

By Eleanor Whitmore • 8 min read • January 8, 2026

As cities expand skyward and horizontal space becomes precious, architects and urban planners are rediscovering the Art Nouveau principle of bringing nature indoors. From cascading vertical gardens in Singapore to rooftop forests in Milan, the integration of living flora into structural design represents more than aesthetic choice—it embodies a fundamental shift in how we conceive urban living spaces.
