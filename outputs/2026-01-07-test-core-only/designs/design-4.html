I've been teaching algorithms for 15 years and I think the fundamentals haven't changed - we still need to emphasize time/space complexity analysis and proof techniques. What HAS changed is the tooling. Students now expect interactive visualizations and immediate feedback.

My workflow: Start with pseudocode on the blackboard (yes, actual blackboard - there's something nostalgic and effective about it), then move to Python notebooks for implementation. The combination of old-school theory and modern execution works well.
