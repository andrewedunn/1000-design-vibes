STANDARD ISSUE

Sourdough Bread

Natural fermentation protocol for artisan bread production

Difficulty MED
Serves 8
Prep Time 48h
Cook Time 45m
Active Time 2h
Category BREAD
Ingredients
Starter Feed
50g Active starter
50g Water (room temp)
50g Bread flour
Dough Components
500g Bread flour
350g Water (filtered, 25°C)
150g Fed starter (active)
10g Sea salt (fine)
Finishing
As needed Rice flour (for dusting)
Required Equipment
  • Large mixing bowl (glass/ceramic)
  • Kitchen scale (digital, 1g precision)
  • Banneton basket or bowl with liner
  • Dutch oven (5-7 quart, cast iron)
  • Bench scraper (metal)
  • Lame or sharp knife
  • Kitchen thermometer
  • Damp towel (lint-free)
Preparation Protocol
01
Feed your sourdough starter 8-12 hours before mixing dough. Combine 50g active starter with 50g water and 50g flour. Mix thoroughly and let sit at room temperature (22-24°C) until doubled in volume and showing vigorous bubbling activity.
TIMING: 8-12 hours passive
02
Autolyse: Mix 500g bread flour with 350g water in a large bowl. Stir until no dry flour remains. Cover with damp towel and rest for 30-60 minutes. This allows flour to hydrate and begins gluten development without mechanical mixing.
TIMING: 30-60 minutes passive
03
Add 150g active starter to the autolyse mixture. Use wet hands to squeeze and fold the starter through the dough until fully incorporated. The dough will be very shaggy and sticky at this stage.
TIMING: 5 minutes active
04
Sprinkle 10g salt over the dough surface. Using the pincer method, pinch salt through the dough, then perform 50-100 slap-and-fold motions until dough becomes smooth and begins to show some tension. Cover and rest for 30 minutes.
TIMING: 10 minutes active
05
Bulk fermentation with stretch-and-folds: Over the next 4 hours, perform 4 sets of stretch-and-folds at 30-minute intervals. For each set, wet hands, stretch one side of dough up and fold over center. Rotate bowl 90° and repeat 4 times. Cover between sets.
TIMING: 4 hours with periodic attention
06
Pre-shape: Turn dough onto lightly floured surface. Use bench scraper to gently shape into a round. Let rest uncovered for 20-30 minutes to relax gluten and allow surface to dry slightly.
TIMING: 20-30 minutes passive
07
Final shape: Flip dough over so smooth side is down. Fold bottom third up, then fold left and right sides to center, then roll from top down creating tension. Place seam-side up in rice-flour-dusted banneton. Cover tightly.
TIMING: 5 minutes active
08
Cold proof: Refrigerate covered dough for 12-24 hours. This slow fermentation develops complex flavor and makes the dough easier to score. Longer proofing yields more sour flavor.
TIMING: 12-24 hours passive
09
Preheat: Place Dutch oven with lid in oven and preheat to 250°C (480°F) for 45 minutes minimum. The vessel must be thoroughly heated to create proper oven spring.
TIMING: 45 minutes passive
10
Score and bake: Turn dough onto parchment paper. Score with lame in desired pattern (single slash or cross recommended). Carefully transfer on parchment into hot Dutch oven. Cover and bake 20 minutes at 250°C, then remove lid and bake 25 minutes at 230°C until deep golden brown.
TIMING: 45 minutes active baking
11
Cool completely on wire rack for minimum 2 hours before slicing. Internal temperature should read 93-96°C. Cutting too early will result in gummy crumb structure. Store at room temperature in paper bag or bread box for up to 4 days.
TIMING: 2+ hours passive cooling
Critical Notes & Warnings
⚠
TEMPERATURE CONTROL: Maintain ambient temperature between 22-24°C during bulk fermentation. Warmer temperatures accelerate fermentation and may result in over-proofed dough. Cooler temperatures slow the process.
⚠
STARTER VIABILITY: Only use starter that has doubled in volume and shows vigorous bubbling. A weak or inactive starter will not provide adequate rise and will result in dense bread.
⚠
HYDRATION ADJUSTMENT: Flour absorption varies by brand and humidity. If dough is excessively sticky after step 4, reduce water by 10-20g in future batches. If too stiff, increase water incrementally.
⚠
SAFETY: Dutch oven reaches extreme temperatures. Use heavy oven mitts and place hot vessel on heat-safe surface. Steam release when removing lid can cause burns.
